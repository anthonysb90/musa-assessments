## What it measures

**Domains / scales (8):** Vision & Direction, Decision-Making, Communication, Delegation & Team Building, Emotional & Spiritual Resilience, Integrity & Accountability, Conflict Navigation, Growth & Adaptability.

## How to take it

1. Go to assessments.chchurch.com and open **Leadership Health — Peer Input** (or use a direct/embed link).
2. This instrument is delivered by **invite only** (text or email) as part of the parent assessment. It is not listed publicly.
3. Answer all 8 questions on the 1–5 scale below, 10 per page.
5. Submit. Your report is generated immediately and shown on screen and emailed to you as a link.

## The questions

All 8 items, in order, grouped by domain. This is the full item bank as it runs live.

**Vision & Direction**  (1 items)

1. This leader sets a clear direction and helps people move toward it.

**Decision-Making**  (1 items)

2. This leader makes sound decisions in a timely way.

**Communication**  (1 items)

3. This leader communicates clearly and listens well.

**Delegation & Team Building**  (1 items)

4. This leader hands off real responsibility and builds up the team.

**Emotional & Spiritual Resilience**  (1 items)

5. This leader stays steady and grounded under pressure.

**Integrity & Accountability**  (1 items)

6. This leader is trustworthy and owns their mistakes.

**Conflict Navigation**  (1 items)

7. This leader handles conflict directly and fairly.

**Growth & Adaptability**  (1 items)

8. This leader keeps growing and adapts well when things change.


## How it is scored

**Type:** Co-rater input. This instrument is **not taken standalone** and is **not published** on the public site. It feeds the **Leadership Health** report.

1. Sent by invite (text or email) to the peer.
2. Answers use the 1–5 scale across 8 areas: Vision & Direction, Decision-Making, Communication, Delegation & Team Building, Emotional & Spiritual Resilience, Integrity & Accountability, Conflict Navigation, Growth & Adaptability.
3. Each area score is the **average** of its items.
4. These averages are placed **alongside the candidate/leader's own scores** in the parent report, so self-view and outside-view sit side by side and blind spots become visible.

### Worked example

If the leader rates their own 'Conflict Navigation' 4.5 but two peers average 3.0, the 1.5 gap is the blind spot the report surfaces.

## The report

There is no separate report for this instrument. Its scores appear inside the **Leadership Health** report as a second (and third) column next to the primary taker's self-assessment. The gap between self-view and observer-view is the insight.

Every report carries the Mission USA credit line: _"Assessment by Mission USA of the Congregational Holiness Church. Get yours at assessments.chchurch.com."_ Reports can be printed or saved as PDF, and are saved to the taker's profile.
