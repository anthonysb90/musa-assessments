## What it measures

The Enneagram describes nine ways people see and move through the world. Over 36 quick either-or choices, this finds the type that fits you best, then turns it toward Christ, with Scripture and a short devotion written to help you grow, not just get a label.

It looks at:

- 9 types, from the Reformer to the Peacemaker
- Your top three, so a close call is never forced
- A redemptive read: your gift, your watch-out, and where to grow

## How it is scored

**Type:** Forced-choice type count.

1. Each of the 36 items is an either/or pick. Value 0 = chose statement A, 1 = chose statement B.
2. Statement A scores toward one type, statement B toward another (see the key in the question list).
3. Each pick adds one point to the chosen statement's type.
4. All nine types are ranked by their point totals; ties break by type number.
5. The highest is the **core type**; the **top three** are highlighted so a close call is never forced into a single label.

### Worked example

Across 36 forced choices, if Type 2 is picked 7 times, Type 9 six times, Type 6 five times → core = Type 2; top three = 2, 9, 6.

## The report

The report names the **core type** (e.g., '2 · The Helper'), shows **all nine types scored with the top three highlighted**, and for the core type gives a redemptive, Christ-centered profile: essence, the gift it brings the body, the watch-out, how it grows, a Scripture, and a short written devotion.

Every report carries the Mission USA credit line: _"Assessment by Mission USA of the Congregational Holiness Church. Get yours at assessments.chchurch.com."_ Reports can be printed or saved as PDF, and are saved to the taker's profile.
