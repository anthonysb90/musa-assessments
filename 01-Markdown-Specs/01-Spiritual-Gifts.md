## What it measures

Spiritual Gifts looks at 25 gift areas drawn from Romans 12, 1 Corinthians 12, and Ephesians 4. You'll answer 125 short statements, and your report ranks every gift from strongest to least, so you can see clearly where you're wired to serve.

It looks at:

- 25 gifts across Romans 12, 1 Corinthians 12, and Ephesians 4
- Your dominant and supporting gifts
- A full ranked profile, not just a single label

**Gifts (25):** Gift A — Prophecy, Gift B — Pastor, Gift C — Teaching, Gift D — Wisdom, Gift E — Knowledge, Gift F — Exhortation, Gift G — Discerning of Spirits, Gift H — Giving, Gift I — Helps, Gift J — Mercy, Gift K — Missionary, Gift L — Evangelist, Gift M — Hospitality, Gift N — Faith, Gift O — Leadership, Gift P — Administration, Gift Q — Miracles, Gift R — Healing, Gift S — Tongues, Gift T — Interpretation, Gift U — Voluntary Poverty, Gift V — Singleness, Gift W — Intercession, Gift X — Exorcism, Gift Y — Service.

## How to take it

1. Go to assessments.chchurch.com and open **Spiritual Gifts** (or use a direct/embed link).
2. Enter your name and contact details (used to save your report to your profile and to email you the link).
3. Answer all 125 questions on the 0–3 scale below, 25 per page.
5. Submit. Your report is generated immediately and shown on screen and emailed to you as a link.

## How it is scored

**Type:** Ranked sum. Each of the 25 gifts is scored by the total of its 5 items.

**How a human knows which question belongs to which gift:** in the database, every item carries a single letter in its `gift_letter` field (A through Y). That letter is the gift. The item's `domain` field is intentionally empty for this assessment — do **not** group by domain here, group by `gift_letter`. The full key is below, and each question in the list above is already shown under its gift.

1. Answers use the 0–3 scale (Not at all, Little, Some, Much).
2. Group the 125 items by their `gift_letter` (A–Y). Each gift has exactly 5 items.
3. Each gift's score is the **sum** of its 5 item values (range 0–15). Sum, not average.
4. Rank all 25 gifts from highest total to lowest; ties break alphabetically by letter.
5. The top gifts are the person's strongest; the report shows the full ranking.

### Worked example

If gift C (Teaching) has items 3, 28, 53, 78, 103 answered 3, 3, 2, 3, 3 → sum = 14. If gift J (Mercy) sums to 3, then Teaching ranks far above Mercy. Do this for all 25 gifts and sort.

## The report

The report leads with the taker's **top gifts**, then lists **every gift ranked with a score and a bar**. Each gift entry carries a plain-language definition, a supporting Scripture, and a note on where that gift best serves. The point is a full ranked profile, not a single label.

Every report carries the Mission USA credit line: _"Assessment by Mission USA of the Congregational Holiness Church. Get yours at assessments.chchurch.com."_ Reports can be printed or saved as PDF, and are saved to the taker's profile.

## Data captured

- Contact block: name, email, phone, age band, ministry role, CHC yes/no, church (if any).
- Every individual answer (stored in `responses`), the scored result (`scored_json`), session timing, and source tag.
- Admins can view the full response set per person in the admin CRM. This is not disclosed on the public site.
