## What it measures

Not how it feels from the platform, how it actually is, according to the people leading alongside you. Your whole leadership team answers privately, and the results combine into one honest picture.

It looks at:

- 8 qualities of a healthy church
- Taken by your whole leadership team
- A 3-rater minimum protects anonymity

**Domains / scales (8):** Leadership Development, Every-Member Ministry, Prayer & Spiritual Depth, Clear Systems & Communication, Worship That Connects, Groups & Discipleship, Outward Focus & Evangelism, Unity & Relational Health.

## How to take it

1. Go to assessments.chchurch.com and open **Church Health** (or use a direct/embed link).
2. A leader starts a **team link** and shares it. Each team member answers **anonymously**. Results unlock at a 3-rater minimum.
3. Answer all 40 questions on the 1–5 scale below, 20 per page.
5. Submit. Your report is generated immediately and shown on screen and emailed to you as a link.

## The questions

All 40 items, in order, grouped by domain. This is the full item bank as it runs live.

**Leadership Development**  (5 items)

1. Our church is intentionally raising up new leaders, not just relying on the same few people.
2. Leaders here are trained and released, not just recruited to fill a gap.
3. I can name someone in our church who has grown into a leadership role in the last year.
4. Our pastor invests real time in developing other leaders, not just doing ministry himself.
5. We have a clear next step for someone who wants to grow into leadership here.

**Every-Member Ministry**  (5 items)

6. Most people in our church are actively serving somewhere, not just attending.
7. New people are helped to discover their gifts and find a place to serve.
8. Our church treats every member's ministry as valuable, not just the platform roles.
9. People are given real responsibility here, not just busywork.
10. I know what my own gifts are and where they fit in this church.

**Prayer & Spiritual Depth**  (5 items)

11. Prayer is a real, active part of how our church makes decisions, not a formality.
12. Our people are growing in their personal walk with God, not just attending services.
13. Our church regularly sets aside dedicated time for prayer, not just mentioning it from the platform.
14. I sense real spiritual hunger in our congregation, not just routine.
15. Our leadership models a genuine devotional life, not just a busy ministry schedule.

**Clear Systems & Communication**  (5 items)

16. People know where to go and who to ask when they have a question or a need.
17. Information flows clearly from leadership to the congregation, not just top-down announcements with no follow-through.
18. Our systems, like check-in, follow-up, and communication, actually work, not just exist on paper.
19. New people are followed up with quickly and consistently.
20. Our church's structure helps ministry happen, rather than slowing it down.

**Worship That Connects**  (5 items)

21. Our worship services genuinely connect people with God, not just fill an hour.
22. Guests and newcomers can follow and engage with our services without feeling lost.
23. Our worship reflects excellence and preparation, not last-minute effort.
24. People leave our services encouraged and changed, not just entertained.
25. Our preaching is clear, biblical, and applicable to daily life.

**Groups & Discipleship**  (5 items)

26. Most of our people are in some kind of ongoing relational group, not just Sunday attendance.
27. Real discipleship, not just information, is happening in our groups.
28. People in our groups know each other well enough to notice when someone is struggling.
29. Our groups are actively multiplying or starting new ones, not staying static.
30. New people are welcomed and assimilated into a group quickly.

**Outward Focus & Evangelism**  (5 items)

31. Our church regularly reaches people who don't yet know Christ, not just transfers from other churches.
32. Our people are equipped and confident to share their faith outside of church walls.
33. Our church invests real resources, time, money, and people, into reaching our community.
34. We measure our success partly by who we're reaching, not just who's already here.
35. Outreach is something our whole church does, not just a designated team.

**Unity & Relational Health**  (5 items)

36. Conflict in our church gets addressed directly and biblically, not avoided or allowed to fester.
37. Our leadership team is unified and trusts each other.
38. People in our church genuinely love and care for one another, not just tolerate each other.
39. Our church has healed well from past conflict or hurt, not just moved past it quietly.
40. New people are welcomed warmly and quickly feel like they belong.


## How it is scored

**Type:** Anonymous multi-rater aggregate.

1. The whole leadership team answers **privately and anonymously** via a shared team link. No login, no individual report.
2. Answers use the 1–5 scale. Items grouped into 8 qualities: Leadership Development, Every-Member Ministry, Prayer & Spiritual Depth, Clear Systems & Communication, Worship That Connects, Groups & Discipleship, Outward Focus & Evangelism, Unity & Relational Health.
3. Individual responses are **not** readable back; a SECURITY DEFINER function recomputes the aggregate.
4. Each quality's team score is the **average across all raters**. A **3-rater minimum** protects anonymity before results show.
5. The aggregate also surfaces where raters **disagree most** (spread), which points to blind spots.

### Worked example

If five leaders rate 'Outward Focus' 2,3,2,3,3 → team average 2.6 (weakest). High spread on a quality flags a blind spot.

## The report

The team report shows a **radar of all eight qualities**, calls out the **weakest area first**, and highlights where the team **sees things differently**. Because it is anonymous and aggregate, no individual answer is ever exposed.

Every report carries the Mission USA credit line: _"Assessment by Mission USA of the Congregational Holiness Church. Get yours at assessments.chchurch.com."_ Reports can be printed or saved as PDF, and are saved to the taker's profile.
