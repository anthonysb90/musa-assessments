## What it measures

Ministry puts a weight on a marriage most never carry. Called Together is an honest look at how yours is holding up, taken privately by each spouse, then shown side by side.

It looks at:

- 8 domains, from Shared Calling to Support & Community
- Taken separately by each spouse
- A confidential safety check, never shared

**Domains / scales (9):** Shared Calling, Boundaries: Home vs Ministry, Role & Expectations, Connection & Intimacy, Conflict & Communication, Margin, Rest & Finances, Criticism & Resilience, Support & Community, Safety.

## How to take it

1. Go to assessments.chchurch.com and open **Called Together** (or use a direct/embed link).
2. One spouse starts it, creating a shared **couple link**. Each spouse then completes it **privately and separately**.
3. Answer all 41 questions on the 1–5 scale below, 20 per page.
5. Submit. Your report is generated immediately and shown on screen and emailed to you as a link.

## The questions

All 41 items, in order, grouped by domain. This is the full item bank as it runs live.

**Shared Calling**  (5 items)

1. We both feel personally called to this ministry, not just one of us.
2. We regularly talk about where we sense God leading us next.
3. I believe my spouse fully supports the ministry work we do together.
4. We make major ministry decisions together, not alone.
5. I would choose this calling again if I could start over.

**Boundaries: Home vs Ministry**  (5 items)

6. We protect at least one full day a week that ministry does not touch.
7. Our home has clear limits on when church needs can interrupt family time.
8. My spouse and I agree on how many nights a week ministry can take us away from home.
9. We turn off ministry calls and messages during family time.
10. Our vacations and days off are rarely interrupted by church needs.

**Role & Expectations**  (5 items)

11. I understand what the church expects of me as a ministry spouse.
12. Those expectations were something we agreed to together, not something assumed of me.
13. I feel free to say no to a ministry role I am not gifted or called for.
14. My spouse defends me when the congregation's expectations of me are unfair.
15. We have talked honestly about which ministry tasks are mine to carry and which are not.

**Connection & Intimacy**  (5 items)

16. We spend meaningful time together that has nothing to do with ministry.
17. We pray for each other, not only for the ministry.
18. Physical affection between us has not been crowded out by ministry demands.
19. I know how my spouse is really doing, not just how they look from the outside.
20. We still pursue each other the way we did earlier in our relationship.

**Conflict & Communication**  (5 items)

21. When we disagree, we can say so honestly, even about ministry decisions.
22. We resolve conflict privately before it ever becomes visible to the church.
23. I feel safe telling my spouse when something they did hurt me.
24. We do not let unresolved conflict linger for days without addressing it.
25. Being public figures does not stop us from having real conversations at home.

**Margin, Rest & Finances**  (5 items)

26. We keep a regular rhythm of rest that ministry does not override.
27. Money stress from ministry income does not regularly strain our marriage.
28. I recognize the signs when my spouse is running on empty.
29. We have said no to ministry opportunities to protect our health.
30. We have a plan, not just a hope, for our financial future.

**Criticism & Resilience**  (5 items)

31. When someone criticizes my spouse's ministry, it does not create distance between us.
32. We process criticism together rather than letting one of us carry it alone.
33. Public criticism of our ministry has not made us more guarded with each other.
34. We remind each other that criticism does not define our worth.
35. A hard season in ministry has not become a hard season in our marriage.

**Support & Community**  (5 items)

36. We have someone outside our own church who speaks into our marriage.
37. We have friends who know us apart from our ministry titles.
38. We are willing to ask for help when our marriage needs it.
39. We have a mentor couple or counselor we could call if things got hard.
40. We do not carry the weight of ministry and marriage entirely alone.

**Safety**  (1 items)

41. Do you feel physically or emotionally safe in your marriage right now?  _(not scored — validity/attention)_


## How it is scored

**Type:** Paired domain averages (couple), with a quarantined safety check.

1. Each spouse takes the assessment **privately**, with no login, via a shared couple link.
2. Answers use the 1–5 scale. Items grouped into 8 marriage-and-ministry domains: Shared Calling, Boundaries: Home vs Ministry, Role & Expectations, Connection & Intimacy, Conflict & Communication, Margin, Rest & Finances, Criticism & Resilience, Support & Community.
3. Each spouse gets a domain average per domain.
4. The couple report places **both spouses' scores side by side** per domain. The couple band for a domain is the **lower** of the two views (the more cautious read).
5. A single confidential **Safety** item is split out before anything is scored or stored on the couple record. A low answer (≤ 2) never appears in the report; instead it shows the answering spouse a private support message and quietly alerts the Mission USA care contact.

### Worked example

If one spouse rates 'Shared Calling' 4.6 and the other 4.0, the couple band uses 4.0. The largest spouse-to-spouse gap is highlighted.

## The report

The couple report shows **both scores side by side** across all eight domains, names shared strengths and shared growth areas, and gently surfaces the **single biggest gap** between the two. The confidential safety answer is never shown to the spouse or in any report.

Every report carries the Mission USA credit line: _"Assessment by Mission USA of the Congregational Holiness Church. Get yours at assessments.chchurch.com."_ Reports can be printed or saved as PDF, and are saved to the taker's profile.

## Data captured

- Contact block: name, email, phone, age band, ministry role, CHC yes/no, church (if any).
- Every individual answer (stored in `responses`), the scored result (`scored_json`), session timing, and source tag.
- Admins can view the full response set per person in the admin CRM. This is not disclosed on the public site.
- Confidential module answers (wellbeing / safety) are stored **separately**, owner-and-care-team only, and never appear in shared reports, exports, or emails.
