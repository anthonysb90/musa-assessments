## 2. Landing page (complete copy)

**Hero**
How deep do your roots go?
Spiritual growth isn't about looking mature. It's about being rooted, so that when the wind comes, you don't move. Rooted helps you see where you're deeply planted and where your roots still need to go down.
Button: Start the Assessment

**About this assessment**
This is not a test you can pass or fail. It's a mirror. Over 40 short statements, you'll reflect honestly on eight areas of spiritual maturity drawn straight from Scripture. The results show you where you're deeply rooted and where you're still growing, with a clear next step either way.

**How it works**
1. Answer 40 statements honestly, about 12 to 15 minutes.
2. See your results across all 8 markers.
3. Get a plain-language look at where you're strong, where to focus next, and one Scripture and one practical step for growth.

**What you'll learn**
Which of the 8 markers of spiritual maturity you're strongest in, which ones need attention, and one concrete next step to grow where it matters most.

**Why it matters**
"Rooted and built up in him and established in the faith" (Colossians 2:7). Maturity in Christ isn't automatic and it isn't accidental. It comes from paying attention to where your roots actually are, not where you assume they are.

## 4. Full item bank (40 items, 5 per marker)

Scale for all items: 1 Strongly Disagree · 2 Disagree · 3 Neutral · 4 Agree · 5 Strongly Agree

**Marker 1 — Abiding in Christ (John 15)**
1. I stay spiritually connected to Christ throughout my normal day, not just in church settings.
2. When I feel distant from God, I notice it and take steps to reconnect.
3. My relationship with Jesus feels alive, not routine or empty.
4. I depend on Christ for my daily strength, not just my own effort.
5. I regularly sense God's presence in ordinary moments, not only in worship services.

**Marker 2 — Word & Prayer**
6. I read Scripture on a regular basis, not only when I'm preparing to teach or preach.
7. My prayer life includes listening to God, not only asking Him for things.
8. Scripture regularly shapes decisions I make, not just my beliefs.
9. I have a consistent time set aside for prayer and the Word.
10. When life gets busy, prayer and Scripture are among the first things I protect, not the first things I drop.

**Marker 3 — Fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5)**
11. People who know me well would say my character has grown over the last year.
12. I respond to conflict with patience more often than I used to.
13. I can name specific ways I have grown in love, joy, or peace recently.
14. Self-control shows up in my daily habits, not just my public behavior.
15. I am quicker to show kindness and gentleness than I was a year ago.

**Marker 4 — Community & Accountability**
16. I have people in my life who know the real me, not just my public role.
17. Someone has permission to ask me hard questions and expect an honest answer.
18. I am currently in real community, not just attending church services.
19. I have confessed a struggle to someone else in the last year.
20. I would notice, and be bothered, if I drifted from accountability.

**Marker 5 — Serving & Gifts**
21. I am actively using my gifts to serve others, not just aware of what they are.
22. Serving feels like an offering to God, not an obligation I'm fulfilling.
23. I look for ways to serve outside of my formal ministry role.
24. I have said yes to serving even when it wasn't convenient.
25. I recognize the gifts in others and encourage them to use their gifts.

**Marker 6 — Witness & Mission**
26. I have shared my faith with someone outside the church in the last year.
27. I pray regularly for specific people who don't know Christ.
28. My life outside of church reflects the same faith I profess inside it.
29. I look for natural opportunities to talk about what God is doing in my life.
30. I care personally about people coming to know Christ, not just about church growth numbers.

**Marker 7 — Stewardship**
31. I give financially in a way that reflects trust in God, not just leftover margin.
32. I manage my time in a way that honors God's priorities, not just my own.
33. I am a faithful steward of what God has given me, even when no one is watching.
34. I make financial decisions with eternity in mind, not just comfort.
35. I have said no to something I wanted so I could be faithful with what I already have.

**Marker 8 — Perseverance in Trials**
36. My faith has grown stronger through hard seasons, not just survived them.
37. I keep trusting God even when I don't understand what He's doing.
38. I have walked through a real trial in the last few years and can point to what it taught me.
39. I don't abandon spiritual practices just because life gets difficult.
40. I have hope that endures even when circumstances don't improve quickly.

## 5. Scoring model

- **Marker score:** average of that marker's 5 items, range 1.0 to 5.0.
- **Bands:**
  - 4.0 to 5.0 = **Deeply Rooted**
  - 2.75 to 3.99 = **Growing**
  - Below 2.75 = **Needs Watering**
- **No overall grade.** Spiritual maturity isn't a single number. Results are shown per marker only.
- **Top 2 strengths:** the two highest-banded markers, named and affirmed first.
- **Bottom 2 growth markers:** the two lowest-banded markers, surfaced with a Scripture anchor and a practical next step each.

Tone note: "Needs Watering" is deliberately not "Weak" or "Failing." Every grower has roots still going down somewhere. That's the plant metaphor doing its job, not a euphemism to soften bad news.

## 6. Results page

1. **Snapshot** — all 8 markers, color-tagged, radar chart.
2. **Where you're deeply rooted** — top 2 markers, named and affirmed.
3. **Where to grow** — bottom 2 markers, each with:
   - a plain-language description of what the score suggests,
   - one Scripture anchor,
   - one concrete, doable next step, not a vague call to "try harder."
4. **Next steps** — scaled to the results, always framed as growth, never as failure.
5. **Download the Rooted Report** — PDF button.

## 9. Data and governance

- **Three tiers**, same pattern as the rest of the hub:
  - *Tier 1 (individual):* per-item responses, per-marker scores, completion timestamp, optional intake fields.
  - *Tier 2:* not applicable, this is a single-respondent instrument.
  - *Tier 3 (aggregate, anonymized, consent-gated):* weakest markers movement-wide, segment patterns by ministry role or years walking with Christ, trend over time if retaken. Feeds curriculum decisions for discipleship resources, same way Called Together's aggregate feeds marriage retreat planning.
- **Never a credentialing gate.** Same rule as every other hub instrument. MIP or leadership may see that someone completed Rooted, never their scores, without the person's own choice to share it.
