## 2. Landing page (complete copy)

**Hero**
Your marriage is carrying more than most. Let's see how it's really doing.
Ministry puts a weight on a marriage that most marriages never carry: public scrutiny, unpredictable hours, a calling one spouse can feel more than the other. Called Together is a honest look at how your marriage is holding up under that weight, built for the two of you to take together.
Button: Start as a Couple

**About this assessment**
This is not a test you can pass or fail. It's a mirror, for both of you. You'll each answer the same 40 statements privately, about 10 minutes each, and then see your results side by side. The gap between your answers is often the most useful part of the whole report.

**How it works**
1. Choose your mode: Health Check if you're already serving, Readiness if you're entering ministry or going through credentialing.
2. Each spouse takes the assessment separately and privately. Answers are not visible to the other spouse until both are done.
3. Once both are complete, you get the Called Together Couple Report together, built to talk through side by side.

**What you'll learn**
Where your marriage is strong, where it needs attention, and the one domain where the two of you see things most differently. Every result comes with a next step, not just a score.

**Why it matters**
"Though one may be overpowered, two can defend themselves. A cord of three strands is not quickly broken." (Ecclesiastes 4:12). Ministry couples don't get to opt out of Scripture's picture of marriage. This assessment exists to help you live it out under real pressure, not just in theory.

Button: Start as a Couple
Privacy note: Your individual answers are never shown to your spouse. Only the combined report is shared, and it goes to the two of you before anyone else.

## 3. Start experience

**Step 1: Mode select**
- Health Check — "We're already serving and want an honest check-in."
- Readiness — "We're entering ministry, planting, or going through MIP."

**Step 2: Intake (per couple, captured once)**
- Both spouses' first and last names, emails (required)
- Church name, district (optional, consent-gated)
- Years in ministry, ministry role of each spouse, church size, ministry stage (optional, consent-gated, powers Tier 3 aggregate data, see Section 9)

**Step 3: Invite the other spouse**
Three inclusion paths, same pattern as Church Planter's spouse assessment:
- **Email invite** — a link is sent to the second spouse to complete on their own time.
- **Device handoff** — "Hand the device to your spouse now" screen, with a private-entry lock so the first spouse's answers can't be seen or edited.
- **Send later** — generates a resumable link, session held open per Section 10 autosave standard.

**Step 4: Each spouse's private session**
- 40 scored items, one screen per statement, 1 to 5 agreement scale.
- Progress bar with milestone encouragement (Section 10), shown per-spouse, never showing the other spouse's progress or answers.
- Final unscored item: the Safety Check (Section 3a).

### 3a. Safety Check (mandatory, unscored, non-negotiable)

Presented once, at the end of each spouse's individual session, before results are calculated. Not part of any domain, not scored into any average, never shown in any report.

**Prompt:** "Do you feel physically or emotionally safe in your marriage right now?"
**Options:** Yes / No / I'd rather not answer

- If **Yes** or **I'd rather not answer**: proceed normally to the standard results flow.
- If **No**: the standard flow is skipped entirely for that spouse. They are routed instead to a private, calm resource screen:

> "Thank you for being honest. Your safety matters more than this assessment. If you are in danger, please contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline at 1-800-799-7233, available 24/7, or text START to 88788. If you would like to talk to someone at Mission USA confidentially, [contact link]. You are not required to finish this assessment, and nothing you've entered will be shared with your spouse."

- This response is never merged into the couple report, never visible to the other spouse, and never routed to a district leader, supervisor, or MIP file. It is visible only to the Mission USA care contact designated in the admin, exactly as the Pastor Profile wellbeing check is quarantined. See Section 9.

## 4. Full item bank (40 items, 5 per domain)

Scale for all items: 1 Strongly Disagree · 2 Disagree · 3 Neutral · 4 Agree · 5 Strongly Agree

**Domain 1 — Shared Calling**
1. We both feel personally called to this ministry, not just one of us.
2. We regularly talk about where we sense God leading us next.
3. I believe my spouse fully supports the ministry work we do together.
4. We make major ministry decisions together, not alone.
5. I would choose this calling again if I could start over.

**Domain 2 — Boundaries: Home vs Ministry**
6. We protect at least one full day a week that ministry does not touch.
7. Our home has clear limits on when church needs can interrupt family time.
8. My spouse and I agree on how many nights a week ministry can take us away from home.
9. We turn off ministry calls and messages during family time.
10. Our vacations and days off are rarely interrupted by church needs.

**Domain 3 — Role & Expectations**
11. I understand what the church expects of me as a ministry spouse.
12. Those expectations were something we agreed to together, not something assumed of me.
13. I feel free to say no to a ministry role I am not gifted or called for.
14. My spouse defends me when the congregation's expectations of me are unfair.
15. We have talked honestly about which ministry tasks are mine to carry and which are not.

**Domain 4 — Connection & Intimacy**
16. We spend meaningful time together that has nothing to do with ministry.
17. We pray for each other, not only for the ministry.
18. Physical affection between us has not been crowded out by ministry demands.
19. I know how my spouse is really doing, not just how they look from the outside.
20. We still pursue each other the way we did earlier in our relationship.

**Domain 5 — Conflict & Communication**
21. When we disagree, we can say so honestly, even about ministry decisions.
22. We resolve conflict privately before it ever becomes visible to the church.
23. I feel safe telling my spouse when something they did hurt me.
24. We do not let unresolved conflict linger for days without addressing it.
25. Being public figures does not stop us from having real conversations at home.

**Domain 6 — Margin, Rest & Finances**
26. We keep a regular rhythm of rest that ministry does not override.
27. Money stress from ministry income does not regularly strain our marriage.
28. I recognize the signs when my spouse is running on empty.
29. We have said no to ministry opportunities to protect our health.
30. We have a plan, not just a hope, for our financial future.

**Domain 7 — Criticism & Resilience**
31. When someone criticizes my spouse's ministry, it does not create distance between us.
32. We process criticism together rather than letting one of us carry it alone.
33. Public criticism of our ministry has not made us more guarded with each other.
34. We remind each other that criticism does not define our worth.
35. A hard season in ministry has not become a hard season in our marriage.

**Domain 8 — Support & Community**
36. We have someone outside our own church who speaks into our marriage.
37. We have friends who know us apart from our ministry titles.
38. We are willing to ask for help when our marriage needs it.
39. We have a mentor couple or counselor we could call if things got hard.
40. We do not carry the weight of ministry and marriage entirely alone.

**Item 41 — Safety Check** (unscored, see Section 3a)

## 5. Scoring model

- **Domain score (per spouse):** average of that domain's 5 items, range 1.0 to 5.0.
- **Domain bands** (applied to the lower of the two spouses' scores, never the average):
  - 4.0 to 5.0 = **Strength**
  - 2.75 to 3.99 = **Steady**
  - Below 2.75 = **Needs Attention**
- **Gap:** absolute difference between spouses' domain scores. A gap of 1.0 or more is flagged as a **notable gap**, regardless of how high both scores are individually.
- **No overall grade.** Results are per-domain only. A single combined number invites misuse as a gate, which Section 9 rules out entirely.
- **Top 2 growth domains:** the two domains with the lowest banded score (ties broken by widest gap), surfaced first in the report.
- **Biggest gap domain:** the single domain with the largest point spread between spouses, called out on its own even if both individual scores look fine.

Why the lower score decides the band, not the average: averaging hides the spouse who is struggling. If one partner rates Boundaries a 5 and the other a 2, the couple does not have a 3.5 marriage on Boundaries. They have a problem one of them is not seeing.

## 6. Results page (on-screen)

Shown only once both spouses have completed. Structure:

1. **Snapshot** — all 8 domains, color-tagged (green Strength, yellow Steady, red Needs Attention), both spouses' scores shown side by side as paired bars.
2. **Where you're strong** — top 2 domains, named and affirmed first.
3. **Where to focus** — bottom 2 domains, each with a plain-language explainer, the gap size and direction, one Scripture anchor, and two conversation questions.
4. **The biggest gap** — called out on its own with the same explainer format, even if it isn't one of the bottom 2 by score.
5. **Next steps** — scaled to results: keep going / talk it through together / reach out for support. Never alarmist.
6. **Support resources** — always shown, quietly, regardless of score.
7. **Download the Couple Report** — PDF button.
8. **Give toward Mission USA marriage resources** — the platform-wide donation feature (Section 10), shown once, not repeated.

Tone throughout: developmental and pastoral. Every growth area is framed as a starting point, never a failure. No score is ever labeled "failing."

## 9. Data and governance (non-negotiable)

- **Results go to the couple first, always.** No supervisor, district leader, or MIP grader sees an individual couple's report without the couple's explicit action to share it.
- **Never a credentialing gate.** MIP and Readiness screening may see that a couple *completed* Called Together, never their scores or answers. A low score cannot be used to fail a couple out of a credentialing track. This must be enforced in the data model, not just the copy.
- **Safety Check responses are quarantined.** Visible only to the designated care contact. Never merged into aggregate data, never visible to the spouse, never part of any export.
- **Three data tiers:**
  - *Tier 1 (individual):* per-item responses, per-domain scores, completion timestamp, optional intake fields, mode taken.
  - *Tier 2 (couple):* per-domain gap, full couple profile, top 2 growth domains, biggest gap, completion status of both spouses, retake history.
  - *Tier 3 (aggregate, anonymized, consent-gated):* weakest domains movement-wide, segment patterns (ministry stage, church size, district, years served), trend over time, cohort read for MIP/Readiness intake shaping curriculum. Never traceable to an individual couple.
- **Aggregate use cases:** design retreats and curriculum around domains that are actually weakest instead of guessing, give district leaders a regional health read without exposing any couple, build an early-warning signal for burnout clusters, feed the broader CHC ministry-health dataset.
