# CHC Church Planter Assessment — Complete Test Package

One file, build-ready. Landing page through emails, buildable in one pass.
Original instrument built on the public 13-characteristic church-planter framework. Every question is written fresh, so the instrument is yours to own and license to other denominations. Designed to a world-class standard: behavioral, validity-checked, spouse-inclusive, and rich in what the candidate walks away with.

## 2. Landing page (complete copy)

**Hero**
Are you ready to plant?
A clear, honest look at your readiness to plant a church, built on decades of research into what actually makes planters succeed.
Button: Begin the Assessment

**About this assessment**
Planting a church is not the same as leading one. It takes a specific set of strengths, and the honest truth is that most plants that struggle, struggle for reasons that were visible beforehand. This assessment looks at thirteen characteristics that consistently mark effective planters, through your own answers, your spouse's perspective, and a trained assessor's interview. The result is not a verdict. It's clarity: where you're strong, where to grow, and what to talk through before you go.

**If you're married**
Your spouse takes part too, with their own honest assessment. Planting is a family calling, and the couples who thrive are the ones who go in with open eyes together.

**How long it takes**
Your part, about 15 to 20 minutes. Your spouse's part, about 8 to 10 minutes. The assessor interview is scheduled separately. You can pause and resume anytime.

**What you'll walk away with**
A rich, personal report: where you'll excel, what to watch for, things to think and pray on, and concrete steps to grow. If you're married, an honest read on where you and your spouse are aligned and where you need to talk.

**Why it matters**
The goal isn't to pass or fail you. It's to set you and your family up to plant well, or to point you toward the role where you'll have the greatest kingdom impact.

**Before you begin**
Answer honestly, based on what you have actually done and where you actually are, not on what you hope to become. Honesty here is a gift to your future ministry.

## 4. Self-assessment item bank (expanded, with validity layer)

Scale 1 to 5. Reverse items marked [R]. Paraphrase consistency pairs marked [P]. Reverse items are recoded before scoring. Validity items (Section 5) are interspersed, unlabeled.

**1. Visioning Capacity** (Primary)
- [P] I have started something from nothing and seen it grow.
- [P] I have launched a ministry, group, or venture that did not exist before I began it.
- I can describe a future that doesn't exist yet in a way that makes others want to be part of it.
- People have joined an effort mainly because of a picture of the future I gave them.
- [R] I am better at maintaining what already exists than starting something new.

**2. Intrinsically Motivated** (Primary)
- [P] I keep working hard toward a goal even when no one is checking on me.
- [P] I stay driven on a project even with no supervision or accountability at all.
- I have pushed through long, hard seasons of work because the goal mattered to me.
- I hold my own work to a higher standard than others require of me.
- [R] I tend to lose steam on goals unless someone is holding me accountable.

**3. Creates Ownership of Ministry** (Primary)
- [P] People I have led took real ownership, not just followed my instructions.
- [P] Those I lead end up owning the work themselves rather than just doing what I say.
- I have handed off real responsibility and watched others carry it well.
- I have trained someone who went on to lead others.
- [R] I find it hard to let go of control of things I care about.

**4. Reaches the Unchurched** (Primary)
- [P] I regularly build genuine friendships with people who don't go to church.
- [P] Several of my real friendships are with people who are far from God.
- People far from God seem comfortable around me.
- I have personally helped someone outside the church move toward faith in Christ.
- [R] Almost everyone I spend time with is already a churchgoer.

**5. Spousal Cooperation** (Primary, married only)
- [P] My spouse and I are united in the call to plant.
- [P] My spouse is as committed to planting as I am.
- We have talked honestly about what planting will cost our family, and we are agreed.
- Our marriage is healthy enough to carry the strain of a plant.
- [R] My spouse has real hesitations about planting that we have not resolved.

**6. Effectively Builds Relationships** (Secondary)
- [P] People find me easy to approach and relate to.
- [P] Others generally find me easy to get along with.
- I make and keep friendships across different kinds of people.
- People tend to open up to me fairly quickly.
- [R] I find it difficult to build close relationships.

**7. Committed to Church Growth** (Secondary)
- [P] I actively want the ministries I lead to grow, not just stay steady.
- [P] I am driven to see the ministries I lead reach more people, not just hold steady.
- I have changed my methods in order to reach more people.
- I keep learning new approaches to ministry.
- [R] I am content when a ministry stays about the same size year to year.

**8. Responsive to the Community** (Secondary)
- [P] I have shaped ministry around the actual needs of a specific community.
- [P] I have built ministry around a community's real, specific needs.
- I pay attention to what a community needs before deciding what to offer.
- I have met a concrete community need as a way into relationship.
- [R] I tend to run the same ministry approach regardless of the community.

**9. Utilizes the Giftedness of Others** (Secondary)
- [P] I help people discover what they are good at and place them there.
- [P] I put people in roles that match their strengths.
- I have built a team by matching people to their gifts.
- I would rather equip others than try to do it all myself.
- [R] When I need something done well, I usually just do it myself.

**10. Flexible and Adaptable** (Secondary)
- [P] I adjust my plans when the situation changes, without losing the goal.
- [P] When circumstances shift, I adapt my plan while keeping the mission.
- I stay effective when things don't go as planned.
- I can hold my plan loosely while holding the mission firmly.
- [R] I get thrown off when things don't go the way I planned.

**11. Builds Group Cohesiveness** (Secondary)
- [P] I have started small groups that became close and lasting.
- [P] Groups I have started tend to become tight-knit and stay together.
- People I bring together tend to bond.
- I know how to move a group from strangers to a team.
- [R] Groups I lead tend to stay loose and disconnected.

**12. Resilience** (Secondary)
- [P] I have recovered and kept going after a real ministry setback.
- [P] After a real failure or setback, I bounce back and keep moving.
- Criticism and failure don't keep me down for long.
- I have stayed steady through a discouraging stretch.
- [R] Setbacks and criticism tend to keep me down for a long time.

**13. Exercises Faith** (Secondary)
- [P] I have stepped out on something only God could accomplish and trusted Him for it.
- [P] I have taken a real step of faith that depended on God coming through.
- My walk with God is the real engine behind my ministry.
- I have believed God for provision or breakthrough and seen Him come through.
- [R] My ministry runs mostly on my own ability rather than active faith.

## 5. Validity layer (unlabeled, interspersed)

**Candor scale** (endorsing = presenting an idealized self):
- I have never exaggerated my role in something that went well.
- I always follow through on every single commitment I make.
- I have never avoided a difficult conversation I should have had.
- I never feel discouraged in ministry.
- I have never let a teammate down.
- I have never spoken about someone in a way I later regretted.
Score: Agree/Strongly Agree count. 0-1 candid, 2-3 moderate concern, 4+ high concern.

**Attention checks:**
- Directed: "To show you are reading carefully, please select Disagree for this item."
- Infrequency: "I have succeeded at everything I have ever attempted." (strong agreement flags careless responding)

**Consistency and style (computed):** paraphrase-pair gaps over 2 points and reverse-item contradictions count toward a Consistency Index (0-2 consistent, 3-5 some, 6+ high). Straight-lining, sub-floor completion time, and extreme responding are flagged.

Validity flags are assessor-only, never disqualify, and direct the interview. Full scoring per the platform validity engine.

## 6. Spouse element (married only) — fully built

This is central to the assessment. The spouse takes their own independent assessment. Their honest view is often the truest signal in the whole process.

### 6.1 Two ways to include the spouse

- **Invite by email:** the candidate enters the spouse's email (at start or at the end). The system emails the spouse a private link to their own assessment, taken independently.
- **Handoff after:** when the candidate finishes, a screen offers "Hand this device to your spouse now" and launches the spouse assessment directly.
- **Send later:** the candidate gets a shareable spouse link at the end to pass along.

In all three, the spouse's assessment is a separate session linked to the candidate's as a couple. The candidate's report generates when they finish; the spouse-alignment section fills in once the spouse completes (shows "pending" until then).

### 6.2 Spouse assessment — Section 1: Your view (calling, marriage, cost)
Scale 1 to 5. Framed for the spouse's honest voice.
- I feel personally called to plant, not just supportive of my spouse's call.
- I am genuinely excited about planting, not only willing to go along with it.
- My spouse and I have talked honestly about what planting will cost our family.
- We are agreed on what our family is willing to sacrifice for this.
- Our marriage is strong enough right now to carry the strain of a plant.
- I am clear on what my own role would be in the plant.
- We have a real plan for protecting our marriage and family during the plant.
- I have people beyond my spouse who will support me through this.
- [R] I have real hesitations about planting that we have not fully worked through.
- [R] I sometimes feel more like a passenger in this call than a partner.

### 6.3 Spouse assessment — Section 2: How you see your spouse
The spouse rates the candidate on characteristics a spouse can genuinely observe. Scale 1 to 5.
- My spouse keeps working hard toward goals even when no one is watching.
- My spouse builds genuine friendships easily, including with people far from God.
- My spouse connects naturally with people outside the church.
- My spouse bounces back from setbacks and criticism.
- My spouse adjusts well when plans change.
- My spouse's faith in God is the real engine behind their ministry.
- My spouse helps other people take ownership and grow as leaders.
- People are naturally drawn to follow my spouse.

### 6.4 Spouse alignment analysis (the payoff)
- **Shared calling alignment:** compare the candidate's Spousal Cooperation answers with the spouse's Section 1. Where the candidate is confident and the spouse is hesitant, that gap is the single most important finding in the assessment. Reported as Aligned / Some gaps to discuss / Significant gaps, with the specific gaps named.
- **Blind-spot check:** compare the candidate's self-ratings with the spouse's Section 2 ratings on the shared characteristics. Large gaps surface where the candidate sees themselves differently than the person closest to them.
- These feed the couple's report section and the assessor's interview probes.

## 7. Assessor rubric (behavioral interview)

For each characteristic: original behavioral questions plus a 1-to-5 anchored score. Scored on concrete past examples, not opinion. Two assessors recommended; average their scores. Anchors: 1 = little or no evidence in past behavior, 3 = some evidence, 5 = strong, repeated track record.

1. Visioning Capacity: Tell me about a time you started something that did not exist. What did you do first, and what happened?
2. Intrinsically Motivated: Tell me about a goal you pursued over a long time with no one supervising you.
3. Creates Ownership: Tell me about someone you developed who then led on their own.
4. Reaches the Unchurched: Tell me about a real friendship with someone outside the church, and how it formed.
5. Spousal Cooperation (both present): In your own words, each of you, tell me about the call to plant, and what worries each of you most.
6. Builds Relationships: Tell me about a relationship you built with someone very different from you.
7. Committed to Growth: Tell me about a time you changed how you did ministry in order to grow it.
8. Responsive to Community: Tell me about a specific community need you identified and how you responded.
9. Utilizes Giftedness: Tell me about a time you placed someone in a role that fit their gift.
10. Flexible: Tell me about a time your plan fell apart. What did you do?
11. Group Cohesiveness: Tell me about a group you formed that became genuinely close.
12. Resilience: Tell me about your hardest ministry failure. What happened next?
13. Exercises Faith: Tell me about a step of faith you took that you could not guarantee.

## 8. Scoring and readiness tiers

- Trait scores from all keyed items, reverse recoded, averaged. Assessor and spouse-observed scores tracked alongside self.
- Weighted composite: primaries 2x, secondaries 1x. Single candidates drop Spousal Cooperation, weight redistributes across the other primaries.
- The primary five carry the readiness decision.
- Self vs assessor vs spouse gaps reported per characteristic.

**Readiness tiers (developmental, not a verdict):**
| Tier | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Ready to Plant | Strong across primaries, spouse aligned, no major flags. |
| Develop First | Real promise with specific primary or alignment gaps to grow before planting. |
| Better Suited Elsewhere For Now | Primary or marriage-alignment gaps suggest lead planting isn't the current fit. Often a strong fit for a planting team or an existing-church role. Framed as fit, not failure. |

Readiness informs the conversation. It never auto-approves or auto-rejects.

## 9. The report (world-class, what they walk away with)

Premium, on the shared engine. Candidate version, couple version, and assessor version. Sections:

1. **Cover** with name, date, marital status.
2. **Readiness snapshot:** 13-spoke radar, primary five marked. Self plotted with assessor and spouse-observed scores where available.
3. **Readiness tier**, with a plain explanation of what it means and does not mean.
4. **Where You'll Excel:** your strongest characteristics, each with how to lean into it in a plant. Affirming and specific.
5. **What to Watch For:** your risk areas and blind spots, including where you rate yourself higher than your spouse or assessor does, and any weak primaries. Framed as things to steward, not failures.
6. **Things to Think and Pray On:** reflection prompts drawn from your growth areas and your sense of calling. Slower, honest questions to sit with.
7. **Things to Implement:** concrete next steps and a development plan tied to your sending organization's training (default CHC and DCPI principles), scaled to your readiness tier.
8. **Marriage and Calling Alignment** (married only): where you and your spouse see things the same, where you see them differently, and specific things to talk through together. Led gently, honestly.
9. **Scripture anchors:** Matthew 28:18-20; Acts 13:1-3; Ephesians 4:11-13; 1 Corinthians 3:6-9; Nehemiah 2.
10. **Assessor appendix** (assessor version only): validity read, self-vs-others gaps, and interview probes for flagged characteristics.

## 11. Data captured and aggregate value

- Per-characteristic self, assessor, and spouse-observed scores, gaps, weighted composite, readiness tier, validity flags, spouse alignment read, intake context, completion time.
- **Aggregate value:** where candidates across a movement are consistently strong or weak, whether spouses are aligned or hesitant as a pattern, and whether training moves the weak characteristics over time. Feeds Complete the Commission '33 and, for other denominations, their own planting pipeline.

---

## 12. Governance

- Developmental, not a gate. Informs the candidate, spouse, and assessing team. Never auto-approves or auto-rejects.
- Spouse results are the couple's, handled with dignity. The spouse's honest hesitations are surfaced for the couple's conversation, not weaponized.
- Validity flags are assessor-only and never disqualify.
- Language is fit and growth, never pass or fail.
- Standard consent, security, RLS, and retention apply. Spouse sessions are linked but each person's raw answers are protected.
