# Leadership Health — A Self and Team Leadership Assessment
## Complete Test Package

One file, build-ready. Landing page through emails. Positioned as the accessible, hub-native leadership check, self-assessment first with an optional lightweight peer layer. Distinct from The Who System on your own site, which is the deep, multi-instrument, research-backed 360 platform. This one is meant to be quick and low-friction for any CHC leader; Who System is the heavier tool for a serious leadership development engagement. Keeping them at different depths is what keeps them from being the same product twice.

How healthy is your leadership, really?
Not how it feels from the inside. Leadership Health gives you an honest self-assessment across 8 areas every leader needs, with the option to add a few trusted voices who see you differently than you see yourself.

**About this assessment**
This is not a test you can pass or fail. It's a mirror, and if you choose to invite others, a second mirror held up next to yours. If you invite at least 3 people who know your leadership to weigh in, you'll also see where they see you differently than you see yourself, which is often where the real growth is.

Where you're strongest and where you need attention, according to your own honest read, and if you add peer input, where you might be blind to something others see clearly, or underselling a strength others already recognize in you.

"The way of a fool is right in his own eyes, but a wise man listens to advice" (Proverbs 12:15). Self-assessment is a start. It isn't the whole picture. This tool is built to give you both.

## 3. Start experience

**Step 1: Self-assessment (always taken first)**
- Name, email, church, district, ministry role (name/email required, rest optional and consent-gated)
- 40 items, one screen per statement, 1 to 5 scale
- Progress bar with milestone encouragement (Section 10)

**Step 2: Results shown immediately** (self-only view, Section 6)

**Step 3: Optional peer invitation**
- After seeing self-results, the leader is offered: "Want a second perspective? Invite 3 or more people who know your leadership."
- Leader enters names/emails of peers (staff, board members, spouse, mentor, whoever they choose).
- Each peer receives a private link with the same 40 items reframed as observations about the leader (Section 4a).
- Leader can see how many peers have responded (a count only) until the 3-rater minimum unlocks the comparison view.

## 4. Full item bank — self version (40 items, 5 per domain)

Scale for all items: 1 Strongly Disagree · 2 Disagree · 3 Neutral · 4 Agree · 5 Strongly Agree

**Domain 1 — Vision & Direction**
1. I have a clear sense of where I'm leading people, not just what needs to get done today.
2. I communicate vision often enough that people around me could repeat it in their own words.
3. I make time to think ahead, not just react to what's urgent.
4. People who follow me understand why we're doing what we're doing, not just what to do.
5. I revisit and adjust our direction when circumstances change, rather than defaulting to the original plan out of habit.

**Domain 2 — Decision-Making**
6. I make decisions in a reasonable amount of time, not paralyzed by indecision.
7. I gather the right input before deciding, without letting that become an excuse to delay.
8. I own the outcome of my decisions, good or bad, rather than shifting blame.
9. I am willing to make an unpopular decision when it's the right one.
10. I don't let fear of criticism keep me from deciding what needs to be decided.

**Domain 3 — Communication**
11. People understand what I'm asking of them the first time I say it.
12. I listen as much as I talk in conversations that matter.
13. I communicate difficult things directly, not through hints or through someone else.
14. I follow through on what I say I'll communicate or follow up on.
15. I adjust how I communicate depending on who I'm talking to, not just my own default style.

**Domain 4 — Delegation & Team Building**
16. I give real responsibility to others, not just tasks I don't want to do myself.
17. I trust the people I've delegated to, rather than micromanaging their work.
18. I am actively developing someone who could eventually replace me in my role.
19. My team feels ownership over their work, not just compliance with my instructions.
20. I recognize and credit others' contributions publicly, not just privately.

**Domain 5 — Emotional & Spiritual Resilience**
21. I recover from setbacks in a reasonable amount of time, not carrying them for weeks.
22. My mood and energy are steady enough that people around me aren't managing my emotions.
23. I have a consistent practice, spiritual or otherwise, that keeps me grounded under pressure.
24. I know the early warning signs when I'm running on empty, and I take them seriously.
25. I don't take criticism of my leadership as an attack on my identity.

**Domain 6 — Integrity & Accountability**
26. What I do in private matches what I say and teach in public.
27. I have someone in my life who has permission to challenge my decisions.
28. I admit when I'm wrong quickly, without needing to be cornered into it.
29. I keep commitments even when it's inconvenient or costly to do so.
30. I am honest about my own weaknesses with the people closest to me, not just my strengths.

**Domain 7 — Conflict Navigation**
31. I address tension directly instead of hoping it resolves on its own.
32. I can disagree with someone and still maintain the relationship afterward.
33. I stay calm enough in conflict to think clearly, not just react.
34. I follow up after a conflict to make sure it's actually resolved, not just quieted.
35. I am willing to have the hard conversation instead of avoiding the person.

**Domain 8 — Growth & Adaptability**
36. I am actively working on a specific weakness right now, not just aware that I have one.
37. I seek out feedback rather than waiting for it to be offered.
38. I have changed my mind about something significant in the last year based on new information.
39. I invest time in my own growth, not just my ministry's growth.
40. I adapt my leadership approach as the situation or the people change, rather than leading the same way regardless of context.

### 4a. Peer version (same 40 items, reframed as third-person observation)

Every item above is rewritten with "This leader..." in place of "I...", same domains, same order, same 1 to 5 scale. Example: item 1 becomes "This leader has a clear sense of where they're leading people, not just what needs to get done today." Applied mechanically across all 40 items, no new content to write, just the pronoun and verb-agreement swap.

## 5. Scoring model

- **Self domain score:** average of that domain's 5 self items, range 1.0 to 5.0.
- **Self bands:**
  - 4.0 to 5.0 = **Strength**
  - 2.75 to 3.99 = **Steady**
  - Below 2.75 = **Needs Attention**
- **Peer domain score** (only computed once 3+ peers have responded): average of that domain's 5 peer items across all peer raters.
- **Self-vs-Peer comparison, once unlocked:**
  - **Blind Spot:** self-score notably higher than peer average (gap of 0.75 or more on the 1–5 scale). The leader sees themselves stronger in this domain than others experience them.
  - **Hidden Strength:** self-score notably lower than peer average (same 0.75 gap, reversed). The leader is underselling a strength others already see.
  - **Aligned:** gap under 0.75. Self and peer perception roughly agree.
- **No single overall grade.** Results are per-domain, both self and comparison.

## 6. Results page

**Immediate (self-only, shown right after the self-assessment):**
1. Snapshot — radar of all 8 domains, self-scores only.
2. Top 2 strengths, named and affirmed.
3. Bottom 2 growth domains, each with a plain-language description and one practical next step.
4. Invitation to add peer input, framed as optional and valuable, never required.

**Comparison view (unlocked once 3+ peers respond):**
5. Self vs. peer radar, both lines shown together.
6. Blind Spot domains called out explicitly, this is usually the most useful part of the report.
7. Hidden Strength domains called out, since underselling a strength is its own kind of leadership limitation.
8. Aligned domains listed briefly, confirmation is still useful information.
9. Growth plan built around the widest gaps, not just the lowest scores.
10. Download the Leadership Health Report — PDF button.
11. Give toward Mission USA leadership development — the platform-wide donation module (Section 10).

## 9. Data and governance

- **3-peer minimum before any comparison is shown**, same anonymity floor used in Church Health and modeled on the Who System's dial anonymity rule.
- **Individual peer identity and individual peer scores are never shown to the leader**, only the combined peer average per domain.
- **Three data tiers:**
  - *Tier 1 (individual):* self responses, self domain scores, completion timestamp, optional intake fields.
  - *Tier 2 (self + peer):* peer domain averages, self-vs-peer gaps, Blind Spot / Hidden Strength / Aligned tags. Individual peer responses never surfaced.
  - *Tier 3 (aggregate, anonymized, consent-gated):* which domains CHC leaders most commonly overestimate or underestimate in themselves, segment patterns by role or years in ministry. Feeds what leadership training actually needs to cover, not what's assumed to be needed.
- **Never a credentialing gate.** A Blind Spot is information for growth, never a disqualifier, and peer input is never visible to MIP graders or supervisors.
