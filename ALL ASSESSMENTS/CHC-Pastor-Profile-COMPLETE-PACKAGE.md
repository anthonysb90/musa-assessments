# Pastor Profile Assessment — Complete Test Package

One file, build-ready. Landing page through emails.
Original instrument built on the public Character / Competence / Contribution frame plus the biblical qualifications of 1 Timothy 3 and Titus 1. Designed world-class: honest, affirming, blind-spot revealing, and safe on the sensitive parts.

Two modes: **Ready** (discerning a call to pastor) and **Health Check** (current pastor, with optional 360). Built to affirm the call, surface what the pastor may not see, and encourage.

**About**
Pastoring asks everything of a person: their character, their gifts, their marriage, their health, and their soul. This assessment looks at all of it, honestly and without judgment. It affirms where you're strong and called, and it gently surfaces the things that are easy to miss when you're the one carrying the load. If you're discerning a call to pastor, it shows you where to grow first. If you're already pastoring, it gives you a clear, encouraging read on how you're really doing.

**Private where it counts**
Part of this assessment is a confidential check on how you're doing personally. Those answers are yours alone. They are never shared with your church or denomination.

**How long**
About 15 to 20 minutes. Pause and resume anytime. Current pastors can optionally invite a few people for a 360, to see what others see.

**What you'll walk away with**
Your calling affirmed, your strengths named, the blind spots others see that you may not, honest things to watch for, things to think and pray on, and concrete steps to grow. Plus a private read on your own wellbeing and rest.

**Why it matters**
You pour out for everyone else. This is a few minutes poured back into you.

Answer honestly, from your real life right now. This is for you.

## 3. Start experience

**Honest-answering instruction (own screen):**
> **Before you begin**

**Start form:** first name, last name, email (required); mode (Ready or Health Check); marital/family status (drives the Marriage & Family domain); role and years in ministry, church, district (optional intake); demographics (optional, consented); consent checkboxes (default off).

**Health Check 360 (optional):** the pastor may invite raters (board members, staff, peers) by email. Raters answer a shorter observable subset. Identities stay anonymous. This is how blind spots surface.

**Wellbeing notice (before that module):**

## 4. Item bank

Scale 1 to 5 unless noted. [R] reverse-keyed. Health Check phrases present tense; Ready phrases forward-looking. Validity items (Section 5) interspersed, unlabeled.

### Pillar 1 — Character & Inner Life

**1. Calling & Conviction**
- I have a clear, settled sense that God has called me to pastoral ministry.
- In hard seasons, my sense of calling has held me steady.
- I can point to ways God confirmed this call through others and circumstances.
- [R] I often question whether I'm really meant to be in ministry.

**2. Character & Integrity**
- My private life matches my public ministry.
- I handle money, truth, and relationships in a way that is above reproach.
- I own my failures quickly rather than hiding them.
- [R] There are areas of my life I would not want the congregation to see.

**3. Spiritual Vitality**
- My walk with God is alive, not just professional.
- I spend regular time with God that has nothing to do with sermon prep.
- My love for Jesus is deeper now than it was a year ago.
- [R] My time with God mostly runs on ministry necessity, not personal hunger.

**4. Emotional Health**
- I am aware of my emotions and handle them in healthy ways.
- I have healthy ways of processing stress and disappointment.
- I let trusted people see how I'm really doing.
- [R] I tend to bury what I feel and push through.
(The deeper wellbeing check is the confidential module, Section 6.)

**5. Rest, Rhythm & Health**
- I take a consistent day off that ministry does not invade.
- I take care of my physical health: sleep, movement, and eating.
- I take my time away and vacation without guilt.
- [R] I am running on empty more often than not.

**6. Marriage & Family (conditional)**
- My marriage is healthy and a source of strength.
- My family gets my best, not just my leftovers.
- My spouse and I are united in the ministry.
- [R] Ministry regularly costs my family more than we have agreed to.

### Pillar 2 — Competence

**7. Preaching & Teaching**
- I preach and teach in a way that helps people understand and grow.
- I handle Scripture faithfully and accurately.
- People tell me my teaching has changed how they live.
- I keep growing as a communicator.

**8. Pastoral Care & Shepherding**
- I genuinely love and care for the people I lead.
- People come to me in crisis and leave helped.
- I balance caring for people with leading them.
- [R] I find it hard to carry people's burdens without being crushed by them.

**9. Leadership & Vision**
- I set a clear direction and move people toward it.
- I develop and release other leaders.
- I can make hard decisions when they are needed.
- People follow my leadership willingly.

**10. Conflict Resolution**
- I face conflict directly rather than avoiding it.
- I stay calm and non-defensive when I'm criticized.
- I help people in conflict move toward real resolution.
- [R] I tend to avoid hard conversations until they blow up.

**11. Communication & Relationships**
- I communicate clearly and listen well.
- I build healthy relationships across the church.
- I keep short accounts and repair relationships quickly.
- [R] People sometimes find me harder to reach than I intend.

### Pillar 3 — Contribution & Support

**12. Disciple-Making & Multiplication**
- I intentionally make disciples, not just attract a crowd.
- I have a clear pathway that moves people toward maturity.
- The people I lead are beginning to reproduce in others.

**13. Mentoring & Accountability** (structured + scaled)
- Do you currently have a mentor, coach, or older voice speaking into your ministry? (Yes / No)
- If yes, how often do you meet? (Weekly / Monthly / A few times a year / Rarely)
- Do you have people who genuinely hold you accountable? (Yes / No)
- Are you currently mentoring or discipling someone yourself? (Yes / No)
- If yes, roughly how many? (number)
- Scaled: I actively seek input from people further along than me.
- Scaled: I am intentionally raising up the next generation of leaders.
- Scaled [R]: I mostly carry ministry alone, without close accountability.

**14. Mission & Evangelism**
- I keep the church focused outward, not just inward.
- I personally engage people who are far from God.
- We are reaching new people, not just circulating existing ones.

## 5. Validity layer (unlabeled, interspersed)

**Candor scale** (endorsing = idealized self): I have never resented a church member. I always practice what I preach, without exception. I have never let ministry crowd out my time with God. I never feel like quitting. I have never handled a conflict poorly.
Score: Agree/Strongly Agree count. 0-1 candid, 2-3 moderate, 4+ high concern.

**Attention checks:** one directed item, one infrequency item.

**Consistency/style (computed):** reverse-item contradictions and straight-lining flagged. Assessor/360-only. Never disqualifies. In an affirming self-assessment, a high candor score is handled gently: the report simply invites the pastor to consider whether they're being fully honest with themselves, never accuses.

## 6. Confidential wellbeing module (private to the pastor)

Its own section, framed by the notice in Section 3. Scale 0 to 3 (Not at all / Several days / More than half the days / Nearly every day), thinking of the last two weeks. Original, non-diagnostic items.

- I've had little interest or joy in things I usually enjoy.
- I've felt down, discouraged, or hopeless.
- I've felt exhausted or drained, even after rest.
- I've felt overwhelmed by the weight of ministry.
- My sleep has been off, too little or too much.
- I've felt alone, like no one really knows what I'm carrying.
- I've felt like I'm just going through the motions with God.
- I've wondered whether I can keep going.

**Bands and response (shown only to the pastor):**
- **Doing okay:** a brief affirming note and an encouragement to keep tending their soul.
- **Some strain:** a caring note naming that this season sounds heavy, with gentle suggestions (rest, a trusted friend, their own pastoral care).
- **Significant strain** (high total, or elevated on the "hopeless" or "keep going" items): a caring, direct message that how they're feeling matters and they don't have to carry it alone, with resources: the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988 in the US) and an encouragement to reach out to a doctor, counselor, or someone they trust. Supportive, never alarming, never a diagnosis.

**Hard rules for the build:**
- Wellbeing answers and result are visible only to the pastor. Never in the 360, never to the church or denomination, never in an identifiable export.
- No diagnosis language anywhere. This is a wellbeing check, not a clinical screen.
- The module never interrogates with safety-assessment questions. On strain, it expresses care and offers resources, and stops there.
- No claims are made about the confidentiality of outside crisis services. The tool simply offers the resource.
- Any aggregate use of wellbeing data is fully de-identified, opt-in, and reported only as broad movement-level percentages, if at all.

## 7. Scoring

- Domain scores from keyed items, reverse recoded, averaged.
- Pillar composites for Character & Inner Life, Competence, and Contribution & Support. The three-pillar balance is a core insight: strong competence with thin character or inner life is the classic pastoral failure pattern, and the pillar view makes it visible.
- Health Check 360: self vs others per domain, surfacing blind spots.
- **Ready mode** adds a readiness picture (developmental, never a calling verdict): Ready to Pastor / Grow First / Explore Further, each framed as guidance, not judgment.
- No overall pass/fail. No score confirms or denies a call.
- Wellbeing module scored separately, private, never folded into the overall profile.

The private wellbeing result. Call to action to download the full report. Behind the scenes: save to CRM (wellbeing excluded from shared data), email the report link.

## 9. The report (world-class, affirming, blind-spot revealing)

Premium. Candidate version and assessor/360 version. Sections:

1. **Cover.**
2. **Your Calling:** an affirming reflection on their call, leading with strength. This sets the tone: this is for them.
3. **Three-pillar snapshot:** radar of all 14 domains, grouped and colored by pillar, plus pillar composites. Self plotted with 360 where available.
4. **Where You'll Excel:** top domains, affirmed, with how to lean into them.
5. **What Others See That You May Not** (360 only): the blind-spot section, where others rate you differently than you rate yourself, delivered gently and constructively.
6. **What to Watch For:** honest risk areas, weak domains, and the classic pressure points (isolation, thin rest, avoided conflict), framed as things to steward.
7. **Things to Think and Pray On:** slower reflection prompts on calling, health, and growth.
8. **Things to Implement:** concrete steps, scaled to their situation, tied to their sending organization's care and development resources.
9. **Your Support System:** a plain read on mentoring and accountability, whether they have a mentor, whether they're mentoring anyone, and a nudge where either is missing. Isolation is the quiet killer in ministry, so this gets its own space.
10. **A Word of Encouragement:** a closing, genuinely encouraging note. They lead a hard life. Send them off strengthened.
11. **Private: How You're Doing** (pastor's copy only): the wellbeing result and any care resources.
12. **Assessor/360 appendix** (that version only): validity read, self-vs-others gaps, probes. Wellbeing is never included here.

## 11. Data captured and aggregate value

- Domain scores (self and 360), pillar composites, readiness picture (Ready mode), validity flags, mentoring and accountability status, completion time.
- **Wellbeing data is quarantined:** private to the pastor, excluded from all identifiable data and standard aggregates.
- **Aggregate value (non-wellbeing):** where CHC pastors and candidates are consistently strong or weak by pillar and domain. This feeds MIP curriculum and continuing-education design. Mentoring and accountability rates across the movement tell you whether pastors are isolated, which is one of the most actionable things you could learn.

---

## 12. Governance

- Never a calling verdict. No score confirms or denies a call.
- Never a credentialing gate. Results go to the pastor first.
- 360 rater identities anonymous.
- Wellbeing module private to the pastor, non-diagnostic, care-routing, quarantined from all sharing.
- Affirming, encouraging tone throughout. This tool exists to strengthen pastors, not audit them.
- Standard consent, security, RLS, and retention apply.
