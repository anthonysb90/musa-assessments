## 2. Landing page (complete copy)

**Hero**
Discover how God has wired you to lead.
Ephesians 4 names five ways God equips His church: apostles, prophets, evangelists, shepherds, and teachers. Fivefold Calling helps you see which of these is your primary lane, and which one is close behind.
Button: Start the Assessment

**About this assessment**
This is not a test you can pass or fail. It's a mirror. Over 25 short statements, you'll reflect honestly on how you naturally lead and serve. The results show your primary and secondary calling, with a plain description of what that means for how you're most useful in the church.

**How it works**
1. Answer 25 statements honestly, about 10 minutes.
2. See your primary and secondary calling, with all 5 scores shown.
3. If your whole team takes it, see the team's composition, which functions you have and which you're missing.

**What you'll learn**
Your primary and secondary calling among the five, described in plain language, along with practical ways that calling shows up and where it can go wrong if it's unchecked.

**Why it matters**
"And he gave the apostles, the prophets, the evangelists, the shepherds and teachers, to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ" (Ephesians 4:11-12). God didn't design one kind of leader. He designed five, working together. Knowing your calling helps you find your seat at that table.

## 4. Full item bank (25 items, 5 per calling)

Scale for all items: 0 Not at all true of me · 1 A little true · 2 Somewhat true · 3 Very true of me

**Pioneering (Apostolic)**
1. I am energized by starting new ministries or reaching new groups of people, rather than maintaining what already exists.
2. I naturally see opportunities others don't see yet.
3. I am willing to leave comfort and stability to go where the need is greatest.
4. People often look to me to get a new initiative off the ground.
5. I would rather build something new than manage something already established.

**Prophetic**
6. I am quick to sense when something isn't right, even if others haven't noticed yet.
7. I feel a strong pull toward truth and calling things what they actually are, even when it's uncomfortable.
8. I often sense direction from God that I feel compelled to share with others.
9. I care more about being faithful to what's right than being liked.
10. People sometimes tell me I see things clearly before anyone else does.

**Evangelistic**
11. I look for natural opportunities to talk with people who don't know Christ.
12. I get excited talking about my faith with someone who has never heard it.
13. I have led someone to Christ outside of a formal church setting.
14. I feel most alive when I'm sharing the gospel with someone new.
15. I can explain the gospel simply, in a way an outsider can understand.

**Shepherding**
16. I am drawn to caring for people through their struggles over the long haul.
17. People come to me when they are hurting, and I make time for them.
18. I notice when someone is missing or drifting, and I follow up.
19. I find deep satisfaction in walking with the same people over years, not just seasons.
20. Protecting and nurturing people matters more to me than moving fast.

**Teaching**
21. I enjoy studying Scripture deeply and explaining it clearly to others.
22. People tell me I help them understand things they didn't understand before.
23. I prepare thoroughly before I teach or explain something important.
24. I care about accuracy and depth, not just making a point land emotionally.
25. I naturally break down complex ideas into steps others can follow.

## 5. Scoring model

- **Calling score:** sum of that calling's 5 items, range 0 to 15.
- **Primary calling:** highest-scoring calling. If two callings tie for highest, both are shown as co-primary rather than forcing a false tiebreak.
- **Secondary calling:** next-highest score. Same tie handling if two callings tie for second.
- **No calling is "better."** All 5 are shown with their scores. The report never implies apostolic or prophetic outranks shepherding or teaching.

---

## 6. Results page

1. **Primary and secondary calling**, named and described first, with a short explanation of what each looks like in ministry.
2. **All 5 scores** shown as a radar chart, so the full profile is visible, not just the top 2.
3. **Where it can go wrong** — one honest paragraph per primary/secondary calling naming the shadow side (for example, Pioneering unchecked can abandon people too quickly; Prophetic unchecked can become harsh; Shepherding unchecked can resist necessary change). This keeps the tool developmental, not just flattering.
4. **Ministry application** — 2 to 3 concrete ways this calling could be used at their church right now.
5. **Team composition view** (only shown if a team code was used): which of the 5 callings are represented on the team and which are missing, without showing any individual's personal scores to teammates.
6. **Download the Fivefold Report** — PDF button.
7. **Give toward Mission USA leadership development** — the platform-wide donation module (Section 9).

## 7. PDF report — The Fivefold Report

CHC/Mission USA branded (Cambria headings, Calibri body, navy/gold/light-blue palette). Contents:

- Cover page with name, primary and secondary calling, and the radar chart.
- Full write-up for all 5 callings, not only the top 2, each with a Scripture reference (Apostolic: Ephesians 2:20, Prophetic: Ephesians 4:11 with 1 Corinthians 14:3, Evangelistic: Acts 8:26-40 as Philip's model, Shepherding: John 10:11-14, Teaching: Ezra 7:10).
- A "How to Use This Calling" page: practical next steps drawn from the results-page ministry application list.

## 9. Data and governance

- **Three tiers**, same pattern as the rest of the hub:
  - *Tier 1 (individual):* per-item responses, per-calling scores, primary and secondary calling, completion timestamp, optional intake fields.
  - *Tier 2 (team):* calling distribution across a team, which functions are present or missing. Individual scores never shown to teammates, only the aggregate composition.
  - *Tier 3 (aggregate, anonymized, consent-gated):* which callings are common or scarce across CHC leadership overall, segment patterns by district or ministry role. Useful for spotting whether CHC is producing enough pioneering and prophetic voices relative to shepherds and teachers, which tend to be overrepresented in most denominations.
- **Never a credentialing gate.** A low score in a particular calling is information, not disqualification.
