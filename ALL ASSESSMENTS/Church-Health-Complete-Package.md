## 2. Landing page (complete copy)

**Hero**
How healthy is your church, really?
Not how healthy it feels from the platform. How healthy it actually is, according to the people leading it alongside you. Church Health gathers your whole leadership team's honest read across 8 areas, so you get the real picture, not just your own.
Button: Start as a Leadership Team

**About this assessment**
This is not a test you can pass or fail. It's a mirror, for your whole team. Each leader answers the same 40 statements privately, about 15 to 20 minutes, and the results combine into one picture of your church's health, including where your team agrees and where it doesn't.

**How it works**
1. The pastor or team lead starts the assessment and invites the rest of the leadership team.
2. Each leader takes it privately. Individual answers are never shown to teammates.
3. Once at least 3 leaders have completed it, the combined Church Health Report is ready.

**What you'll learn**
Which of the 8 areas your church is strongest in, which one needs attention most, and where your leadership team sees things differently from each other, which is often the most useful finding in the whole report.

**Why it matters**
"From him the whole body, joined and held together by every joint with which it is equipped, when each part is working properly, makes the body grow" (Ephesians 4:16). A healthy church isn't the pastor's opinion of the church. It's every part working properly, together.

## 3. Start experience

**Step 1: Team setup (pastor or team lead)**
- Church name, district, pastor name, email (required)
- Generates a shared team link and a team code
- Sets an optional target rater count (defaults to "whole leadership team")

**Step 2: Invite raters**
- Team lead sends the shared link to each leader (elders, staff, board, key volunteers, whoever counts as leadership in that church).
- Each rater enters the team code to join, then completes the assessment privately under their own session.

**Step 3: Individual session**
- 40 items, one screen per statement, 1 to 5 agreement scale.
- Progress bar with milestone encouragement (Section 10).
- Autosave and resume, no account required beyond the team code.

**Step 4: Waiting state**
- Team lead can see how many raters have completed (a count only, never who or what they answered) until the 3-rater minimum is met.

## 4. Full item bank (40 items, 5 per domain)

Scale for all items: 1 Strongly Disagree · 2 Disagree · 3 Neutral · 4 Agree · 5 Strongly Agree

**Domain 1 — Leadership Development**
1. Our church is intentionally raising up new leaders, not just relying on the same few people.
2. Leaders here are trained and released, not just recruited to fill a gap.
3. I can name someone in our church who has grown into a leadership role in the last year.
4. Our pastor invests real time in developing other leaders, not just doing ministry himself.
5. We have a clear next step for someone who wants to grow into leadership here.

**Domain 2 — Every-Member Ministry**
6. Most people in our church are actively serving somewhere, not just attending.
7. New people are helped to discover their gifts and find a place to serve.
8. Our church treats every member's ministry as valuable, not just the platform roles.
9. People are given real responsibility here, not just busywork.
10. I know what my own gifts are and where they fit in this church.

**Domain 3 — Prayer & Spiritual Depth**
11. Prayer is a real, active part of how our church makes decisions, not a formality.
12. Our people are growing in their personal walk with God, not just attending services.
13. Our church regularly sets aside dedicated time for prayer, not just mentioning it from the platform.
14. I sense real spiritual hunger in our congregation, not just routine.
15. Our leadership models a genuine devotional life, not just a busy ministry schedule.

**Domain 4 — Clear Systems & Communication**
16. People know where to go and who to ask when they have a question or a need.
17. Information flows clearly from leadership to the congregation, not just top-down announcements with no follow-through.
18. Our systems, like check-in, follow-up, and communication, actually work, not just exist on paper.
19. New people are followed up with quickly and consistently.
20. Our church's structure helps ministry happen, rather than slowing it down.

**Domain 5 — Worship That Connects**
21. Our worship services genuinely connect people with God, not just fill an hour.
22. Guests and newcomers can follow and engage with our services without feeling lost.
23. Our worship reflects excellence and preparation, not last-minute effort.
24. People leave our services encouraged and changed, not just entertained.
25. Our preaching is clear, biblical, and applicable to daily life.

**Domain 6 — Groups & Discipleship**
26. Most of our people are in some kind of ongoing relational group, not just Sunday attendance.
27. Real discipleship, not just information, is happening in our groups.
28. People in our groups know each other well enough to notice when someone is struggling.
29. Our groups are actively multiplying or starting new ones, not staying static.
30. New people are welcomed and assimilated into a group quickly.

**Domain 7 — Outward Focus & Evangelism**
31. Our church regularly reaches people who don't yet know Christ, not just transfers from other churches.
32. Our people are equipped and confident to share their faith outside of church walls.
33. Our church invests real resources, time, money, and people, into reaching our community.
34. We measure our success partly by who we're reaching, not just who's already here.
35. Outreach is something our whole church does, not just a designated team.

**Domain 8 — Unity & Relational Health**
36. Conflict in our church gets addressed directly and biblically, not avoided or allowed to fester.
37. Our leadership team is unified and trusts each other.
38. People in our church genuinely love and care for one another, not just tolerate each other.
39. Our church has healed well from past conflict or hurt, not just moved past it quietly.
40. New people are welcomed warmly and quickly feel like they belong.

## 5. Scoring model

- **Domain score (church-level):** average of all raters' scores on that domain's 5 items, range 1.0 to 5.0.
- **Domain bands:**
  - 4.0 to 5.0 = **Strength**
  - 2.75 to 3.99 = **Steady**
  - Below 2.75 = **Needs Attention**
- **Team spread (per domain):** the range between the highest and lowest rater average on that domain. A wide spread matters as much as a low score, since it shows the team doesn't see that area the same way.
- **Weakest domain caps overall health.** The single lowest-scoring domain leads the report, regardless of how strong the other 7 are. A church isn't as healthy as its best area, it's as healthy as its weakest one until that gets attention.
- **No single overall grade.** Results are per-domain only.

## 6. Results page

Shown once the minimum rater count is met.

1. **Snapshot** — radar of all 8 domains, church-level averages.
2. **Weakest domain, called out first** — named plainly, with what it suggests and why it leads the report.
3. **Where you're strong** — top 2 domains, affirmed.
4. **Where your team disagrees** — the domain with the widest spread between raters, shown even if its average score looks fine, since disagreement is information the average hides.
5. **Focus plan** — 2 to 3 practical next steps for the bottom 2 domains.
6. **Rater count shown**, never individual identities or individual scores.
7. **Download the Church Health Report** — PDF button.
8. **Give toward Mission USA church health resources** — the platform-wide donation module (Section 10).

## 9. Data and governance

- **Minimum 3 raters required before any report generates.** Below that threshold, individual answers could be reverse-identified by anyone who knows the team. This mirrors the anonymity floor already built into the Who System's 360 dial coloring.
- **Individual rater identity and individual scores are never shown to anyone**, including the pastor or team lead. Only the church-level averages, bands, and spread are visible.
- **Three data tiers:**
  - *Tier 1 (individual, internal only):* per-item responses, used only to compute the aggregate, never surfaced.
  - *Tier 2 (church):* per-domain averages, bands, spread, weakest domain, widest-spread domain.
  - *Tier 3 (aggregate, anonymized, consent-gated):* weakest domains across CHC churches, segment patterns by district or church size, movement-wide health trends. Feeds curriculum and coaching resource decisions the same way the other hub assessments' aggregate data does.
- **Never a credentialing gate or a district ranking tool used punitively.** A low domain score is a call to support the church, not a mark against the pastor.
