## 4. Full item bank (28 items, 7 per dimension)

Scale for all items: 1 Not me at all · 2 A little · 3 Somewhat · 4 Mostly me · 5 Completely me

**Drive (D)**
1. I like being in charge and making the call.
2. I make decisions quickly and move forward without dwelling.
3. I welcome a challenge, especially one people doubt can be done.
4. I get straight to the point instead of talking around an issue.
5. I would rather lead an initiative than wait to be assigned one.
6. I push hard to reach a goal, even when it's uncomfortable for others.
7. I am comfortable taking a calculated risk to get results.

**Influence (I)**
8. I build relationships easily and enjoy meeting new people.
9. I bring energy to a room and lift the mood of a meeting.
10. I can get people excited about a new idea or direction.
11. I would rather talk something through with others than work it out alone.
12. I stay hopeful and look for the upside, even in a hard season.
13. I enjoy encouraging people publicly, not just privately.
14. I use stories and illustrations naturally when I communicate.

**Steadiness (S)**
15. I stay calm when things get stressful or chaotic.
16. I am loyal and stick with people and commitments for the long haul.
17. People know they can depend on me to follow through.
18. I would rather preserve peace than start a conflict, even when I probably should speak up.
19. I prefer steady, predictable routines over constant change.
20. I listen patiently before I respond, even under pressure.
21. I am the person people come to when they need someone calm in the room.

**Conscientiousness (C)**
22. I want to understand all the facts before I make a decision.
23. I catch details and inconsistencies that other people miss.
24. I prefer doing something right over doing it fast.
25. I follow a clear process rather than winging it.
26. I hold myself and others to a high standard of accuracy.
27. I would rather have a well-researched plan than a bold guess.
28. I take time to think things through before I speak.

## 5. Scoring model

- **Dimension score:** sum of that dimension's 7 items, range 7 to 35.
- **Primary:** highest-scoring dimension. **Secondary:** next-highest.
- **Blend code:** primary letter followed by secondary letter (example: Drive primary, Influence secondary = "DI").
- **Tiebreak:** if a tie affects primary or secondary, resolve in order D, I, S, C.
- **All 4 scores shown**, not just the top 2, so the person sees their full wiring, not a flattened label.

## 6. The 12 blends

Each blend pairs with one biblical leader. Structure per profile: strengths, watch-outs, best used for, growth challenge.

**DI — Elijah, The Bold Voice**
Strengths: decisive under pressure, unafraid of public confrontation, rallies people around a clear stand, brings intensity that shakes people out of neutral.
Watch-outs: can crash hard after a big moment (1 Kings 19), can burn relational bridges by moving too fast, may struggle in sustained low-visibility seasons.
Best used for: calling a church back to conviction, leading through crisis, moments requiring a clear public stand.
Growth challenge: Build in a planned rest and debrief after every major public moment, before the crash finds you first.

**DS — Joshua, The Steady Commander**
Strengths: decisive but patient, follows through over the long haul, leads with courage without needing the spotlight, honors the leaders before him.
Watch-outs: can be slow to speak up when patience tips into passivity, may under-communicate vision because action feels like enough.
Best used for: succession seasons, long building projects, leading a team through a multi-year vision.
Growth challenge: Practice narrating your vision out loud at least once a week, not just executing it quietly.

**DC — Nehemiah, The Builder**
Strengths: goal-driven and highly organized, turns a big vision into a workable plan, handles opposition firmly without losing focus, prays first and moves fast.
Watch-outs: can run over people's feelings in pursuit of the plan, may struggle to slow down for those who need more process.
Best used for: capital projects, organizational turnarounds, any season requiring both vision and execution.
Growth challenge: Before your next big push, ask one person how the plan is affecting them personally, and actually wait for the answer.

**ID — Peter, The Passionate Leader**
Strengths: bold, relationally warm, quick to act, brings others along through sheer enthusiasm and conviction.
Watch-outs: can speak or commit before thinking it through, prone to overpromising under emotion and underdelivering under pressure.
Best used for: rallying a team emotionally, pioneering new relationships and partnerships, leading through personal example.
Growth challenge: Practice a 24-hour pause before committing to anything significant, especially when you're excited.

**IS — Barnabas, The Encourager**
Strengths: builds people up consistently, connects others to opportunity, steady and loyal support, sees potential others miss.
Watch-outs: can avoid necessary confrontation to protect the relationship, may struggle to hold someone accountable once they've been encouraged.
Best used for: mentoring emerging leaders, team chaplaincy, any role built on long-term relational investment.
Growth challenge: The next time someone you're encouraging needs correction, give it within the week, not "eventually."

**IC — Miriam, The Expressive Guardian**
Strengths: leads worship and celebration naturally, watches over details and people others overlook, combines public expression with quiet vigilance.
Watch-outs: can let emotion override process in the moment, may struggle when asked to step back from a visible role.
Best used for: worship leadership, protective and pastoral care roles, ministry blending public expression with behind-the-scenes watchfulness.
Growth challenge: Practice handing off a visible role to someone else for a season, and notice what that reveals about your identity.

**SD — Esther, The Patient Strategist**
Strengths: waits for the right moment instead of forcing one, calm under real threat, decisive when the moment finally calls for it.
Watch-outs: can wait too long out of caution, may need a push to act when timing will never feel perfect.
Best used for: high-stakes negotiations, seasons requiring discernment before action, advocacy on behalf of others.
Growth challenge: Set a deadline for your next big decision, so patience doesn't quietly become avoidance.

**SI — Abigail, The Wise Peacemaker**
Strengths: reads a volatile situation clearly, speaks persuasively without escalating conflict, calm and steady under pressure, generous with people.
Watch-outs: can over-function to prevent conflict that isn't actually hers to manage, may absorb the fallout of other people's poor decisions.
Best used for: conflict mediation, diplomacy between leaders or factions, de-escalating tense congregational moments.
Growth challenge: Name one conflict you've been managing that actually belongs to someone else, and hand it back.

**SC — Job, The Enduring Witness**
Strengths: remains faithful through prolonged hardship, reasons carefully rather than reacting emotionally, doesn't abandon conviction under pressure.
Watch-outs: can internalize suffering rather than seeking support, may struggle to ask for help even when clearly needed.
Best used for: walking with people through long-term grief or crisis, roles requiring endurance over years, not months.
Growth challenge: Identify one person you'd let speak into your hardest season, and actually let them.

**CD — Moses, The Reluctant Commander**
Strengths: meticulous about following God's instructions exactly, capable of decisive action when the moment requires it, deeply relational with God even while leading a nation.
Watch-outs: can hesitate at the start out of self-doubt, may become impatient with people once finally moving.
Best used for: high-stakes leadership requiring precision, seasons where getting the details of God's instruction right matters more than moving fast.
Growth challenge: Before your next decisive action, name what specifically you're impatient about, out loud, to someone else.

**CI — Ezra, The Teaching Scholar**
Strengths: deeply prepared and detail-oriented, communicates what he's studied clearly to real audiences, credible because the depth is genuine.
Watch-outs: can over-invest in preparation and under-invest in relationship, may assume understanding transferred just because it was taught well.
Best used for: teaching and curriculum development, credentialing and doctrinal instruction, any role where accuracy and clarity both matter.
Growth challenge: After your next teaching moment, follow up personally with one person instead of assuming the content did the work.

**CS — Daniel, The Principled Diplomat**
Strengths: unwavering conviction paired with genuine relational favor, calm under extreme pressure, consistent in private discipline regardless of who's watching.
Watch-outs: can be slow to act when principle isn't clearly at stake, may avoid necessary boldness by defaulting to careful diplomacy.
Best used for: navigating politically complex environments, holding a consistent standard across a long career, mentoring younger leaders in integrity.
Growth challenge: Identify one place where careful diplomacy has actually become avoidance, and take the direct action instead.

## 8. Platform standards, PDF report, emails, and data governance

Follows the same pattern established across the hub:

- **PDF report:** CHC/Mission USA branded, cover page with blend and biblical parallel, full write-up of the blend, and a "Next 30 Days" page built from the growth challenge.
- **Emails:** report-ready email naming the blend and biblical parallel in the first line, with results link and PDF download.
- **Data tiers:** Tier 1 individual (scores, blend, intake fields), Tier 3 aggregate (which blends are common or scarce across CHC leadership, feeding leadership development planning). No Tier 2, single-respondent instrument.
- **Never a credentialing gate.** A blend is a description of wiring, not a qualification.
- **Silent timing, autosave, resumable magic link, embeddable via `/embed/wired-to-lead`, donation module suppressed for MIP-context sessions.**
